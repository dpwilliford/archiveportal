# Privacy, Consent, and Community Agency

## Foundational principle

The archive does not own community identity. It stewards representations of people, histories, relationships, and materials under negotiated conditions.

## Consent

Consent must be treated as ongoing and revisable.

The system must support changes to:

- names
- pronouns
- identity descriptors
- visibility preferences
- attribution preferences
- access restrictions
- takedown decisions
- representation preferences

## Sensitive classifications

Supported classifications:

- outing risk
- HIV/AIDS sensitivity
- youth-related
- sexually explicit
- harassment risk
- deceased-person sensitivity
- disputed identity
- disputed provenance
- community-sensitive
- uncertain consent

## Design rule

The portal must prioritize stewardship, context, and accountable description over engagement optimization, virality, or sensationalized exposure.
