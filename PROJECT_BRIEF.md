# Sarasota LGBT Archive Archival Operating Environment (AOE)

## Project Name

**Sarasota LGBT Archive Archival Operating Environment (AOE)**

Core software components:

1. Intake Workbench (IWB)
2. Archive Operating System (AOS)
3. Public Portal (PP)

## Executive Definition

The Sarasota LGBT Archive Archival Operating Environment is a standards-aligned, community-governed software and infrastructure platform designed to acquire, preserve, describe, manage, and disseminate the documentary heritage of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer communities in Sarasota, Florida.

The system integrates donor intake, oral history capture, digitization, born-digital transfer, archival description, digital preservation, rights management, and public dissemination within a unified architecture grounded in OAIS, PREMIS, DACS, BagIt, and related standards.

## Mission

To document, preserve, and disseminate the histories, experiences, cultural productions, and community records of LGBT individuals and organizations in Sarasota and the surrounding region through professionally managed archival stewardship and community-centered historical interpretation.

## Design Principles

1. Community ownership and governance
2. Standards-based preservation
3. Offline-first field acquisition
4. Privacy and consent by design
5. Open formats and exportability
6. Preservation of original evidence
7. Separation of preservation masters from public derivatives
8. Accessibility and inclusive description
9. Incremental and modular development
10. Independence from proprietary vendors

## One-Sentence Definition

The Sarasota LGBT Archive Archival Operating Environment is a community-governed archival infrastructure that captures, preserves, and publishes the documentary history of LGBT life in Sarasota through an integrated system for intake, preservation, and public access.
